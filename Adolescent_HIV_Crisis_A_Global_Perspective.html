Executive Summary¶

This report examines adolescent global HIV infection UNICEF data in order to study critical patterns and correlations with development indicators. Finally, we find that Sub-Saharan Africa has the highest burden of infection. As we have discovered strong correlations between socioeconomic factors and HIV infection rates, we have found strong correlation between HIV infection rates and life expectancy and GDP per capita.

Key Findings:¶

  • Progress in the elimination of adolescent HIV infections is being made globally, while in certain regions, adolescent HIV infections persistently remain high.
  • Countries with lower life expectancy and GDP more generally have higher adolescent HIV rates.
  • Indications for the progress of time trends are slow but stable curves of reduction of new infections
  • Interventions aimed in countries with high burden is likely to yield a significant impact at the global level.

Introduction¶

HIV remains a major public health issue worldwide, and ultimately more than 40 million have died from it. Although prevention and treatment have made great progress, adolescents aged 10 to 19 are still a particularly vulnerable segment. It is important to understand the patterns and drivers of HIV infection among this age group to develop effective intervention. This report uses UNICEF data to examine:

  • The geographic distribution of adolescent HIV infections
  • Regional disparities in infection rates
  • Correlations between HIV rates and socioeconomic factors
  • Trends in adolescent HIV infections over time

The purpose of this paper is to offer policy and resource allocation implications rooted in evidence to address this important public health issue.

# Create a function to assign regions based on country names
def assign_region(country):
    africa = ['South Africa', 'Nigeria', 'Kenya', 'Ethiopia', 'Ghana', 'Tanzania',
              'Uganda', 'Zimbabwe', 'Zambia', 'Malawi', 'Botswana', 'Namibia', 'Angola']
    asia = ['India', 'China', 'Japan', 'Thailand', 'Indonesia', 'Vietnam',
            'Philippines', 'Malaysia', 'Pakistan', 'Bangladesh', 'Cambodia']
    europe = ['UK', 'France', 'Germany', 'Italy', 'Spain', 'Russia',
              'Sweden', 'Norway', 'Finland', 'Denmark', 'Poland', 'Ukraine']
    americas = ['United States', 'Canada', 'Brazil', 'Mexico', 'Argentina',
                'Chile', 'Peru', 'Colombia', 'Venezuela', 'Cuba']

    if any(c.lower() in country.lower() for c in africa):
        return 'Africa'
    elif any(c.lower() in country.lower() for c in asia):
        return 'Asia'
    elif any(c.lower() in country.lower() for c in europe):
        return 'Europe'
    elif any(c.lower() in country.lower() for c in americas):
        return 'Americas'
    else:
        return 'Other'

region_hiv = merged_data.copy()
region_hiv['region'] = region_hiv['country'].apply(assign_region)

# Ensure we have clean data for the scatter plot
scatter_data = merged_data.dropna(subset=['Life_expectancy', 'infection_rate'])
print(f"Scatter plot data points available: {len(scatter_data)}")

# Improved regression analysis with log transformation
if len(scatter_data) > 5:  # Need meaningful number of points for regression
    try:
        # Log transformation for better fit
        scatter_data['log_infection_rate'] = np.log1p(scatter_data['infection_rate'])
        scatter_data['log_GDP'] = np.log1p(scatter_data['GDP_per_capita'])

        # Fit regression model
        model = ols('log_infection_rate ~ Life_expectancy + log_GDP', data=scatter_data).fit()
        print("Regression model fitted successfully")

        # Add predictions to data frame
        scatter_data['predicted_rate'] = np.expm1(model.predict(scatter_data))

        # Print regression results
        print("\nRegression Results:")
        print(model.summary().tables[1])  # Print just the coefficient table
    except Exception as e:
        print(f"Error in regression analysis: {e}")
        scatter_data['predicted_rate'] = scatter_data['infection_rate']  # Use actual rate as fallback
else:

Conclusion and Recommendations¶

An analysis of UNICEF adolescent HIV infections data shows that there are significant global disparities which correlate well with socioeconomic factors. There has been progress but many challenges and especially in Sub-Saharan Africa remain.

Key Insights¶

  • Geographic Concentration: HIV infections of adolescents have a geographic pattern of concentration, with the greatest burden falling in Sub-Saharan Africa.
  • Socioeconomic Correlations: The higher the adolescent HIV infection rates, the lower the life expectancy rate and GDP per capita of the countries.
  • Slow Progress: Just as global trends have been moving gradually backward overall, the rate of decline in adolescent HIV infections as a disease is slowing recent years.

Recommendations¶

Based on our analysis, we recommend:

  • Targeted Interventions: Focus resources on high-burden countries identified in this report.
  • Integrated Approach: Address HIV alongside broader socioeconomic development initiatives.
  • Adolescent-Specific Programs: Prevention and treatment programs developed with emphasis in meeting adolescents' specific need and challenge.
  • Improved Data Collection: Improve systems for tracking of adolescent HIV infections to address data gaps in this analysis.
  • Policy Advocacy: These findings can be used by you as a mobilizing force for greater attention and resources given for adolescent HIV prevention and control.
